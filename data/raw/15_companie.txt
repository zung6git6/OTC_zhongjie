<p><b>LG Corporation</b> (or <b>LG Group</b>),<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;a&#93;</a></sup> formerly known as <b>Lucky-Goldstar</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;b&#93;</a></sup> is a South Korean <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Conglomerate_(company)" title="Conglomerate (company)">conglomerate</a> founded by <a href="/wiki/Koo_In-hwoi" title="Koo In-hwoi">Koo In-hwoi</a> and managed by successive generations of his family. It is the fourth-largest <span title="Korean-language text"><i lang="ko-Latn"><a href="/wiki/Chaebol" title="Chaebol">chaebol</a></i></span> (family-run conglomerate) in South Korea. Its headquarters are in the LG Twin Towers building in <a href="/wiki/Yeouido-dong" class="mw-redirect" title="Yeouido-dong">Yeouido-dong</a>, <a href="/wiki/Yeongdeungpo_District" title="Yeongdeungpo District">Yeongdeungpo District</a>, <a href="/wiki/Seoul" title="Seoul">Seoul</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Corporate_Information_3-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Corporate_Information-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> LG makes <a href="/wiki/Electronics" title="Electronics">electronics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Chemical_substance" title="Chemical substance">chemicals</a>, <a href="/wiki/Household_appliance" class="mw-redirect" title="Household appliance">household appliances</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Telecommunications" title="Telecommunications">telecommunications</a> products and operates subsidiaries such as <a href="/wiki/LG_Electronics" title="LG Electronics">LG Electronics</a>, <a href="/wiki/Zenith_Electronics" title="Zenith Electronics">Zenith</a>, <a href="/wiki/LG_Display" title="LG Display">LG Display</a>, <a href="/wiki/LG_Uplus" title="LG Uplus">LG Uplus</a>, <a href="/wiki/LG_Innotek" title="LG Innotek">LG Innotek</a>, <a href="/wiki/LG_Chem" title="LG Chem">LG Chem</a>, and <a href="/wiki/LG_Energy_Solution" title="LG Energy Solution">LG Energy Solution</a> in over 80 countries.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>LG Corporation was established as <a href="/wiki/LG_Chem" title="LG Chem">Lak Hui Chemical Industrial Corp.</a> in 1947 by <a href="/wiki/Koo_In-hwoi" title="Koo In-hwoi">Koo In-hwoi</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> In 1952, Lak Hui (락희) (pronounced "Lucky"; now LG Chem) became the first South Korean company to enter the <a href="/wiki/Plastics_industry" title="Plastics industry">plastics industry</a>. As the company expanded its plastics business, it established <a href="/wiki/GoldStar" title="GoldStar">GoldStar</a> Co. Ltd. (now LG Electronics Inc.) in 1958. Both companies Lucky and GoldStar merged to form Lucky-Goldstar in 1983.<sup id="cite_ref-Song_2017_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Song_2017-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>GoldStar produced South Korea's first <a href="/wiki/Radio_receiver" title="Radio receiver">radio</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Song_2017_7-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Song_2017-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Many <a href="/wiki/Consumer_electronics" title="Consumer electronics">consumer electronics</a> were sold under the brand name GoldStar, while some other household products (not available outside South Korea) were sold under the brand name of Lucky. The Lucky brand was famous for hygiene products such as soaps and HiTi laundry detergents, but the brand was mostly associated with its Lucky and Perioe <a href="/wiki/Toothpaste" title="Toothpaste">toothpaste</a>. LG continues to manufacture some of these products for the South Korean market, such as laundry detergent.
</p><p>Koo In-hwoi led the corporation until his death in 1969, at which time, his son <a href="/wiki/Koo_Cha-kyung" title="Koo Cha-kyung">Koo Cha-kyung</a> took over. He then passed the leadership to his son, <a href="/wiki/Koo_Bon-moo" title="Koo Bon-moo">Koo Bon-moo</a>, in 1995. Koo Bon-moo renamed the company to LG in that year.<sup id="cite_ref-Song_2017_7-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Song_2017-7">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> The company also associates the letters LG with the company's tagline "Life's Good". Since 2009, LG has owned the <a href="/wiki/Domain_name" title="Domain name">domain name</a> LG.com.
</p><p>Koo Bon-moo died of a <a href="/wiki/Brain_tumor" title="Brain tumor">brain tumor</a> on 20 May 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> In July 2018, it was announced that Koo Kwang-mo, the nephew and adopted son of Koo Bon-moo, will be the new CEO of LG. Koo Bon-moo adopted his nephew in 2004, after losing his only son in 1994,<sup id="cite_ref-Kim_2018_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Kim_2018-9">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> citing "a family tradition of male-only succession".<sup id="cite_ref-Lee_2018_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Lee_2018-10">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 31 July 2021, LG Electronics officially ended its mobile phone business, disappearing into history 26 years after its inception.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Businesses">Businesses</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Businesses"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:LG_logo_(2023).svg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/LG_logo_%282023%29.svg/220px-LG_logo_%282023%29.svg.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="220" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/LG_logo_%282023%29.svg/330px-LG_logo_%282023%29.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/a2/LG_logo_%282023%29.svg/440px-LG_logo_%282023%29.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="1000" data-file-height="1000" /></a><figcaption>Secondary logo used since 2023<sup id="cite_ref-LG_Updates_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LG_Updates-11">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup></figcaption></figure>
<p>LG Corporation is a <a href="/wiki/Holding_company" title="Holding company">holding company</a> that operates worldwide through more than 30 companies in the electronics, chemical, and telecom fields. Its electronics subsidiaries manufacture and sell products ranging from electronic and digital home appliances to televisions and mobile telephones, from <a href="/wiki/Thin-film-transistor_liquid-crystal_display" class="mw-redirect" title="Thin-film-transistor liquid-crystal display">thin-film-transistor liquid-crystal displays</a> to security devices and <a href="/wiki/Semiconductor" title="Semiconductor">semiconductors</a>. In the chemical industry, <a href="/wiki/Subsidiaries" class="mw-redirect" title="Subsidiaries">subsidiaries</a> manufacture and sell products including cosmetics, industrial textiles, rechargeable batteries and toner products, <a href="/wiki/Polycarbonate" title="Polycarbonate">polycarbonates</a>, medicines, and surface decorative materials. Its telecom products include long-distance and international phone services, mobile and broadband telecommunications services, as well as consulting and telemarketing services. LG also operates the <a href="/wiki/Coca-Cola" title="Coca-Cola">Coca-Cola</a> Korea Bottling Company, manages real estate, offers <a href="/wiki/Management_consulting" title="Management consulting">management consulting</a>, and operates professional sports clubs.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Joint_ventures">Joint ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=LG&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Joint ventures"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>LG and <a href="/wiki/Hitachi" title="Hitachi">Hitachi</a> created joint ventures named <a href="/wiki/Hitachi-LG_Data_Storage" title="Hitachi-LG Data Storage">Hitachi-LG Data Storage</a> in 2000 and LG Hitachi Water Solutions in 2011; among other partnerships it has had, LG has a long relationship with Hitachi dating back to the early years of Goldstar. Since then Hitachi has transferred technologies for LG's products such as radios, wires, TVs, home appliances, semiconductors, etc. The first joint venture between the two is LG Hitachi, which has been around since 1980s when it was established to import computers to Korea.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>LG had two joint ventures with <a href="/wiki/Royal_Philips_Electronics" class="mw-redirect" title="Royal Philips Electronics">Royal Philips Electronics</a>: <a href="/wiki/LG_Philips_Display" class="mw-redirect" title="LG Philips Display">LG Philips Display</a> and <a href="/wiki/LG_Philips_LCD" class="mw-redirect" title="LG Philips LCD">LG Philips LCD</a>, but Philips sold off its shares in late 2008.<sup id="cite_ref-Display_Shares_Drop_14-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Display_Shares_Drop-14">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2005, LG entered into a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Nortel_Networks" class="mw-redirect" title="Nortel Networks">Nortel Networks</a>, creating <a href="/wiki/Ericsson-LG" title="Ericsson-LG">LG-Nortel Co. Ltd.</a>
</p><p>In 2020, LG and Canadian auto supplier <a href="/wiki/Magna_International" title="Magna International">Magna International</a> launched a joint venture known as LG Magna e-Powertrain. The new joint venture will manufacture components used in <a href="/wiki/Battery-electric_vehicle" class="mw-redirect" title="Battery-electric vehicle">electric cars</a> such as electric motors, inverters and onboard chargers.<sup id="cite_ref-Electric_Car_Gear_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Electric_Car_Gear-15">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
