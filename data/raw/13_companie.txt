<p><b>Alienware Corporation</b> is an American <a href="/wiki/Computer_hardware" title="Computer hardware">computer hardware</a> subsidiary brand of <a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a>. Their product range is dedicated to <a href="/wiki/Gaming_computer" title="Gaming computer">gaming computers</a> and accessories and can be identified by their <a href="/wiki/Extraterrestrial_life" title="Extraterrestrial life">alien</a>-themed designs.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup> Alienware was founded in 1996 by Nelson Gonzalez and Alex Aguila.<sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> The development of the company is also associated with Frank Azor (co-founder),<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> Arthur Lewis (vice president),<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> Joe Balerdi (investment partner),<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and <a href="/wiki/Michael_S._Dell" class="mw-redirect" title="Michael S. Dell">Michael S. Dell</a> (<a href="/wiki/Chief_executive_officer" title="Chief executive officer">CEO</a>). The company's <a href="/wiki/Corporate_headquarters" title="Corporate headquarters">corporate headquarters</a> is located in <a href="/wiki/The_Hammocks,_Florida" title="The Hammocks, Florida">The Hammocks</a>, Miami, Florida.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alienware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Founding">Founding</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alienware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Founding"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Established in 1996 as Saikai of Miami, Inc. by Nelson Gonzalez and Alex Aguila, two childhood friends, Alienware assembles <a href="/wiki/Desktop_computer" title="Desktop computer">desktops</a>, <a href="/wiki/Laptop" title="Laptop">notebooks</a>, <a href="/wiki/Workstation" title="Workstation">workstations</a>, and PC gaming consoles.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> According to employees, the name "Alienware" was chosen because of the founders' fondness for the hit television series <i><a href="/wiki/The_X-Files" title="The X-Files">The X-Files</a></i>, which also inspired the science-fiction themed names of product lines such as <a href="/wiki/Area_51" title="Area 51">Area-51</a>, <a href="/wiki/Wright-Patterson_Air_Force_Base" title="Wright-Patterson Air Force Base">Hangar 18</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Aurora_(aircraft)" title="Aurora (aircraft)">Aurora</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> In 1997, the corporation changed its name to Alienware.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisition_by_Dell">Acquisition by Dell</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alienware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acquisition by Dell"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a> had considered buying the Alienware company since 2002, but did not go through with the purchase until March 22, 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> As a subsidiary, it retains control of its design and marketing while benefiting from Dell's purchasing power, economies of scale, and supply chain, which lowered its operating costs.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_18-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Initially, Dell maintained its competing <a href="/wiki/Dell_XPS" title="Dell XPS">XPS</a> line of <a href="/wiki/Gaming_PC" class="mw-redirect" title="Gaming PC">gaming PCs</a>, often selling computers with similar specifications, which may have hurt Alienware's market share within its market segment.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-XPSScaleDown_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XPSScaleDown-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> Due to corporate restructuring in the spring of 2008, the XPS brand was scaled down, and the desktop line was eliminated, leaving only the XPS notebooks,<sup id="cite_ref-XPSScaleDown_20-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-XPSScaleDown-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> but XPS desktop models had returned by the end of the year.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup>
Product development of gaming PCs was consolidated with Dell's gaming division, with Alienware becoming Dell's premier gaming brand.<sup id="cite_ref-WSJ_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-WSJ-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup> On June 2, 2009, The M17x was introduced as the first Alienware/Dell branded system. This launch also expanded Alienware's global reach from six to 35 countries while supporting 17 different languages.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span id="Computer_systems_models_.28after_acquisition_by_Dell.29"></span><span class="mw-headline" id="Computer_systems_models_(after_acquisition_by_Dell)">Computer systems models (after acquisition by Dell)</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alienware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Computer systems models (after acquisition by Dell)"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Windows_OS-based_consoles">Windows OS-based consoles</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alienware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Windows OS-based consoles"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Alienware announced that it would be releasing a series of <a href="/wiki/Video_game_consoles" class="mw-redirect" title="Video game consoles">video game consoles</a> starting in 2014, aiming to compete with the <a href="/wiki/Sony_Interactive_Entertainment" title="Sony Interactive Entertainment">Sony</a> <a href="/wiki/PlayStation_4" title="PlayStation 4">PlayStation 4</a>, <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a> <a href="/wiki/Wii_U" title="Wii U">Wii U</a>, and the <a href="/wiki/Microsoft" title="Microsoft">Microsoft</a> <a href="/wiki/Xbox_One" title="Xbox One">Xbox One</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-:1_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup> The first version in this series, the Alpha, ran <a href="/wiki/Windows_8.1" title="Windows 8.1">Windows 8.1</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> The operating system and ability to play PC games is what separates the Alpha from the <a href="/wiki/Eighth_generation_of_video_game_consoles" title="Eighth generation of video game consoles">eighth generation of video game consoles</a>. At E3 2016, Alienware announced the second rendition of the Alpha, the Alpha R2. The R2 adds 6th generation <a href="/wiki/Intel" title="Intel">Intel</a> processors, a choice of either <a href="/wiki/AMD" title="AMD">AMD</a>'s <a href="/wiki/Radeon" title="Radeon">Radeon</a> R9 M470X or <a href="/wiki/Nvidia" title="Nvidia">Nvidia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GeForce" title="GeForce">GeForce</a> 960 graphics cards, and support for Alienware's proprietary Graphics Amplifier. It also ships with <a href="/wiki/Windows_10" title="Windows 10">Windows 10</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Graphics_Amplifier">Graphics Amplifier</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alienware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Graphics Amplifier"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>The Graphics Amplifier allows an Alienware laptop to run most full length (or smaller, non-hybrid) desktop <a href="/wiki/GPUs" class="mw-redirect" title="GPUs">GPUs</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup> A proprietary PCIe 3.0 ×4 cable is used instead of the Thunderbolt 3 cable used on most other <a href="/wiki/Graphics_processing_unit#External_GPU_(eGPU)" title="Graphics processing unit">eGPUs</a> .<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Laptops">Laptops</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Alienware&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Laptops"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p><big><b>18 inch</b></big>
</p>
<ul><li><b>M18x</b> (discontinued) – Introduced in 2011, it is considered a replacement for the original M17x design, but with a bigger chassis, a screen up to 18.4 inches (47&#160;cm), dual <a href="/wiki/Mobile_PCI_Express_Module" title="Mobile PCI Express Module">MXM</a> 3.0B GPU support, special keyboard macros, and up to 32<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>GB of DDR3-1600<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>MHz <a href="/wiki/RAM" class="mw-redirect" title="RAM">RAM</a>. Shipped with Intel Sandy Bridge processors and the option of single or dual AMD Radeon 6870M/6970M/6990M <a href="/wiki/Radeon_HD_6000_series" title="Radeon HD 6000 series">Radeon HD 6000 series</a> GPU(s), single or dual Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_500_series" title="GeForce 500 series">GeForce 500 series</a> GPU(s). Factory CPU overclocking was also an available option.</li>
<li><b>M18x-R2</b> (discontinued) – 2012 revision of the M18x; originally shipped with Intel Sandy Bridge processors, later shipped with updated with Intel Ivy Bridge processors, single or dual Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_600_series" title="GeForce 600 series">GeForce 600 series</a> GPU(s), single or dual AMD Radeon HD 7970M <a href="/wiki/Radeon_HD_7000_series" title="Radeon HD 7000 series">Radeon HD 7000 series</a> GPU(s), up to 32<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>GB of DDR3-1600<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>MHz, and optional factory overclock.</li>
<li><b>Alienware 18</b> (discontinued) – 2013 refresh of the M18x; updated with Intel <a href="/wiki/Haswell_(microarchitecture)" title="Haswell (microarchitecture)">Haswell Processors</a>, single or dual Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_700_series" title="GeForce 700 series">GeForce 700 series</a> GPU(s), single or dual AMD Radeon R9 M290X GPU(s), and up to 32<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>GB of DDR3L-1600<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>MHz <a href="/wiki/RAM" class="mw-redirect" title="RAM">RAM</a>, and 1<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>TB RAID 0 SSDs along with facelift with new design. Marketed as "Alienware 18" but listed in some countries as "M18XR3 Viking".<sup id="cite_ref-M18XR3_DA_30-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M18XR3_DA-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li><b>Alienware 18 R2</b> (2014) (discontinued) – 2014 Updated version of the Alienware 18 or "M18x R3"; updated with Intel <a href="/wiki/Haswell_(microarchitecture)" title="Haswell (microarchitecture)">Haswell micro architecture processors</a>, single or dual Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_800_series" class="mw-redirect" title="GeForce 800 series">GeForce 800 series</a> GPU(s), up to 32<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>GB of DDR3-1600<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>MHz, and optional overclock.</li>
<li><b>Alienware 18 R3</b> (2015) (discontinued) – 2015 version was a limited re-release of the previous Alienware 18, with updated dual Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_900_series" title="GeForce 900 series">GeForce 900 series</a> GPUs and up to 32<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>GB of DDR3L-1600<span class="nowrap">&#160;</span>MHz.</li>
<li><b>Alienware m18</b> (2023) – The new version of the Alienware m series featuring 18 inch display, 13th Gen Intel Core / Ryzen 7000 series CPU and Nvidia GeForce RTX 40 Series Laptop GPU.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<p><big><b>17 inch</b></big>
</p>
<ul><li><b>M17x</b> (discontinued) – Introduced in 2009, it is the first laptop released by Alienware after the company was bought by Dell. The name and some of the design is based on the Alienware 17 inch laptop, the Alienware M17.</li>
<li><b>M17x-R2</b> (discontinued) – 2010 Revision of the M17x, adding support for Intel i5 and i7 processors, dual MXM 3.0B graphic cards.</li>

In order of model number: i5-750, i5-760, i7-860, i7-870, i7-875 and i7-880. Sealed liquid cooling units for the processors came factory installed. The R2 used dual channel memory and had dedicated graphics card options including AMD <a href="/wiki/Radeon_HD_5000_series" title="Radeon HD 5000 series">Radeon HD 5000 series</a>, Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_400_series" title="GeForce 400 series">GeForce 400 series</a> and Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_500_series" title="GeForce 500 series">GeForce 500 series</a>. <li><b>Aurora R3</b> (discontinued) – This was the third revision of the Aurora. It was based on Intel's P67 platform (LGA 1155 Socket). Processors included Core i5 and i7 processors only (second generation quad core Sandy Bridge). In order of model number: i5-2300, i5-2400, i5-2500, i5-2500K, i7-2600, i7-2600K. Sealed liquid cooling units for the processors came factory installed. The R3 used dual channel memory and had dedicated graphics card options including AMD <a href="/wiki/Radeon_HD_5000_series" title="Radeon HD 5000 series">Radeon HD 5000 series</a> and <a href="/wiki/Radeon_HD_5000_series" title="Radeon HD 5000 series">Radeon HD 5000 series</a> as well as Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_400_series" title="GeForce 400 series">GeForce 400 series</a> and Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_500_series" title="GeForce 500 series">GeForce 500 series</a>. <li><b>Aurora R4</b> (discontinued) – This is the fourth revision of the Aurora. It is based on Intel's X79 platform (LGA 2011 socket). This model shares identical hardware with the Aurora ALX (R4). Processors include Core i7 processors only (third generation quad core and hexacore Sandy Bridge Extreme). In order of model number: i7-3820, i7-3930K (six core) and i7-3960X (six core). Sealed liquid cooling units for the processors came factory installed. The R4 is the first to use quad channel memory and has Dedicated graphics card options including AMD <a href="/wiki/Radeon_HD_6000_series" title="Radeon HD 6000 series">Radeon HD 6000 series</a> and <a href="/wiki/Radeon_HD_7000_series" title="Radeon HD 7000 series">Radeon HD 7000 series</a> as well as Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_500_series" title="GeForce 500 series">GeForce 500 series</a>. Nvidia <a href="/wiki/GeForce_600_series" title="GeForce 600 series">GeForce 600 series</a> were added later in the year. <p>Alienware monitors use a standard naming convention system for their product names.
</p>
<ul><li>First two characters: Represents that it is an Alienware monitor, typically AW.</li>
<li>Characters three and four: Represents the screen size.</li>
<li>Characters five and six: Represents the release year.</li></ul>
<p>The ending characters represent a mix of features, as follows.
</p>
<ul><li>H=1080p resolution</li>
<li>D=1440p resolution</li>
<li>Q=4K resolution</li>
<li>W=Ultrawide</li>
<li>G=NVIDIA G-Sync support</li>
<li>F=AMD FreeSync support</li></ul>
