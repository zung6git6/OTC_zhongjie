<p><b>Acer Inc.</b> (<span class="rt-commentedText nowrap"><span class="IPA nopopups noexcerpt" lang="en-fonipa"><a href="/wiki/Help:IPA/English" title="Help:IPA/English">/<span style="border-bottom:1px dotted"><span title="/ˈ/: primary stress follows">ˈ</span><span title="/eɪ/: &#39;a&#39; in &#39;face&#39;">eɪ</span><span title="&#39;s&#39; in &#39;sigh&#39;">s</span><span title="/ər/: &#39;er&#39; in &#39;letter&#39;">ər</span></span>/</a></span></span>; <a href="/wiki/Help:Pronunciation_respelling_key" title="Help:Pronunciation respelling key"><i title="English pronunciation respelling"><span style="font-size:90%">AY</span>-sər</i></a>) is a Taiwanese <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> company that produces computer hardware and electronics, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Xizhi_District" title="Xizhi District">Xizhi</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Taipei_City" title="New Taipei City">New Taipei City</a>. Its products include <a href="/wiki/Desktop_computer" title="Desktop computer">desktop</a> PCs, laptop PCs (<a href="/wiki/Laptop" title="Laptop">clamshells</a>, <a href="/wiki/2-in-1_PC" class="mw-redirect" title="2-in-1 PC">2-in-1s</a>, <a href="/wiki/Convertible_laptop" class="mw-redirect" title="Convertible laptop">convertibles</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chromebook" title="Chromebook">Chromebooks</a>), <a href="/wiki/Tablet_computer" title="Tablet computer">tablets</a>, <a href="/wiki/Server_(computing)" title="Server (computing)">servers</a>, storage devices, <a href="/wiki/Virtual_reality" title="Virtual reality">virtual reality</a> devices, displays, <a href="/wiki/Smartphone" title="Smartphone">smartphones</a> and <a href="/wiki/Peripheral" title="Peripheral">peripherals</a>, as well as <a href="/wiki/Gaming_computer" title="Gaming computer">gaming PCs</a> and accessories under its Predator brand. As of 2024,<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> Acer is the <a href="/wiki/Market_share_of_personal_computer_vendors" title="Market share of personal computer vendors">world's sixth-largest personal computer vendor</a> by unit sales.<sup id="cite_ref-4" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the early 2000s, Acer implemented a new business model, shifting from a manufacturer to a designer, marketer, and distributor of products, while performing production processes via contract manufacturers.<sup id="cite_ref-CentRes_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-CentRes-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Currently, in addition to its core IT products business, Acer also has a new business entity that focuses on the integration of cloud services and platforms, and the development of smartphones and wearable devices with value-added <a href="/wiki/Internet_of_things" title="Internet of things">IoT</a> applications.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<style data-mw-deduplicate="TemplateStyles:r1062633282">@media all and (max-width:720px){.mw-parser-output .content .thumb>div:not(.thumbinner){display:flex;justify-content:center;flex-wrap:wrap;align-content:flex-start;flex-direction:column}}body.skin-vector .mw-parser-output div.thumb>div:not(.thumbinner){font-size:94%;text-align:center;overflow:hidden;min-width:100px}body.skin-minerva .mw-parser-output div.thumb>div:not(.thumbinner){margin:0 auto;max-width:100%!important}</style>

<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Mpf-IPo.png" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Mpf-IPo.png/220px-Mpf-IPo.png" decoding="async" width="220" height="108" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Mpf-IPo.png/330px-Mpf-IPo.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/a/ac/Mpf-IPo.png/440px-Mpf-IPo.png 2x" data-file-width="640" data-file-height="315" /></a><figcaption>Micro-Professor MPF-I (1981)</figcaption></figure>
<p>Acer was founded as Multitech in 1976 by <a href="/wiki/Stan_Shih" title="Stan Shih">Stan Shih</a> (<a href="/wiki/Traditional_Chinese_characters" title="Traditional Chinese characters">Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh-Hant">施振榮</span>), his wife Carolyn Yeh, and five others in <a href="/wiki/Hsinchu_City" class="mw-redirect" title="Hsinchu City">Hsinchu City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>. The company began with eleven employees and US$25,000 in capital. Initially, it was primarily a distributor of electronic parts and a consultant in the use of microprocessor technologies. It produced the <a href="/wiki/Micro-Professor_MPF-I" title="Micro-Professor MPF-I">Micro-Professor MPF-I</a> training kit, then two Apple II clones–the <a href="/wiki/Microprofessor_II" title="Microprofessor II">Microprofessor II</a> and <a href="/wiki/Microprofessor_III" title="Microprofessor III">III</a>–before joining the emerging IBM PC compatible market and becoming a significant PC manufacturer. The company was renamed Acer in 1987.
</p><p>In 1998, Acer reorganized into five groups: Acer International Service Group, Acer Sertek Service Group, Acer Semiconductor Group, Acer Information Products Group, and Acer Peripherals Group. To dispel complaints from clients that Acer competed with its own products and to alleviate the competitive nature of the branded sales versus contract manufacturing businesses, the company spun off the contract business in 2000, renaming it Wistron Corporation. The restructuring resulted in two primary units: brand name sales and contract manufacturing. In 2001, the company sold its manufacturing units <a href="/wiki/Benq" class="mw-redirect" title="Benq">BenQ</a> and <a href="/wiki/Wistron" title="Wistron">Wistron</a> in order to focus resources on design and sales.
</p><p>Acer increased worldwide sales while simultaneously reducing its labor force by identifying and using marketing strategies that best utilized their existing distribution channels. In January 2005 <a href="/w/index.php?title=Gianfranco_Lanci&amp;action=edit&amp;redlink=1" class="new" title="Gianfranco Lanci (page does not exist)">Gianfranco Lanci</a><span class="noprint" style="font-size:85%; font-style: normal;">&#160;&#91;<a href="https://zh.wikipedia.org/wiki/%E8%94%A3%E5%87%A1%E5%8F%AF%C2%B7%E8%98%AD%E5%A5%87" class="extiw" title="zh:蔣凡可·蘭奇">zh</a>&#93;</span> became the president. By 2005, Acer employed a scant 7,800 people worldwide. Revenues rose from US$4.9 billion in 2003<sup id="cite_ref-Gianfranco_Tutti_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Gianfranco_Tutti-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup> to US$11.31 billion in 2006.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Acer's North American market shares slipped in the early 2000s, while its European market share rose.<sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In the mid-2000s, consumer notebooks were almost the sole growth drivers for the PC industry, and Acer's exceptionally low overheads and dedication to the channel made it one of the main beneficiaries of this trend.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> Acer grew quickly in Europe in part by embracing the use of more traditional distribution channels targeting retail consumers when some rivals were pursuing online sales and business customers. In 2007, Acer bought <a href="/wiki/Gateway,_Inc." title="Gateway, Inc.">Gateway</a> in the United States and Packard Bell in Europe, and became the third-largest provider of computers and the second largest for notebooks, achieving significant improvement in profitability. Acer has strived to become the world's largest PC vendor in the belief that the goal can help it achieve economy of scale and garner higher margin.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> However, such a reliance on the high-volume, low-value PC market made Acer exposed when buying habits changed.
</p><p>In September 2018, Acer spun-off its smart gadget unit GadgeTek Inc., which produces Leap Beads, smart <a href="/wiki/Prayer_beads" title="Prayer beads">prayer beads</a> that track mantras and footsteps.<sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2019, Acer announced the <a href="/wiki/Esports" title="Esports">esports</a> social platform PLANET9.gg, which aims to provide game analytics, community-organized competitions, and social experiences.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="2013_re-organisation">2013 re-organisation</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: 2013 re-organisation"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>In November 2013, chairman and CEO J.T. Wang and president Jim Wong both resigned due to the company's poor financial performance. Wang had been reportedly due to leave Acer at the end of the year and was supposed to have been succeeded by Wong. Acer co-founder <a href="/wiki/Stan_Shih" title="Stan Shih">Stan Shih</a> took over as board chairman and interim president after the departure of Wang and Wong and began to search for new candidates to assume the roles of CEO and president. On 23 December, Acer named <a href="/wiki/Jason_Chen_(businessman)" title="Jason Chen (businessman)">Jason Chen</a>, then the vice president of worldwide sales and marketing at <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Semiconductor_Manufacturing" class="mw-redirect" title="Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing">Taiwan Semiconductor Manufacturing</a>, as its new president and CEO, effective 1 January.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Acquisitions_and_joint_ventures">Acquisitions and joint ventures</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Acquisitions and joint ventures"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>In 1988, Acer acquired <a href="/wiki/Counterpoint_Computers" class="mw-redirect" title="Counterpoint Computers">Counterpoint Computers</a>.</li>
<li>In 1990, Acer acquired <a href="/wiki/Altos_Computer_Systems" title="Altos Computer Systems">Altos Computer Corporation</a>.</li>
<li>In 1993, Acer acquired the PC division of <a href="/wiki/Commodore_International" title="Commodore International">Commodore International</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-16" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In 1997, Acer acquired <a href="/wiki/Texas_Instruments" title="Texas Instruments">Texas Instruments</a> notebook computer business.</li>
<li>On 27 August 2007, Acer announced plans to acquire its US-based rival <a href="/wiki/Gateway,_Inc." title="Gateway, Inc.">Gateway, Inc.</a> for US$710 million. Acer's former chairman, J.T. Wang, stated that the acquisition completed Acer's "global footprint, by strengthening [its] United States presence".<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> Included in this acquisition was the <a href="/wiki/EMachines" title="EMachines">eMachines</a> brand.</li>
<li>In January 2008, Acer announced that it had acquired a controlling interest of 75% of <a href="/wiki/Packard_Bell" title="Packard Bell">Packard Bell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In March 2008, Acer acquired Taiwanese electronics manufacturing company <a href="/wiki/E-TEN" title="E-TEN">E-TEN</a>.</li>
<li>In 2009, Acer acquired 29.9% of Italian computer system manufacturer <a href="/wiki/Olidata" title="Olidata">Olidata</a>.</li>
<li>In August 2010, Acer and <a href="/wiki/Founder_Technology" title="Founder Technology">Founder Technology</a> signed a memorandum of mutual understanding to strengthen their long-term PC business cooperation.</li>
<li>In July 2011, Acer bought iGware Inc. (formally BroadOn)<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup> for US$320 million. The aim is to create Acer Cloud to enter cloud computing market.<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup> iGware had developed cloud software and infrastructure tools for devices. It had also notably partnered with <a href="/wiki/Nintendo" title="Nintendo">Nintendo</a> on several projects.<sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In September 2015, Acer acquired GPS cycling computer brand Xplova.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In September 2015, Acer invested in robotics start-up company <a href="/wiki/Cynthia_Breazeal" title="Cynthia Breazeal">Jibo</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In March 2016, Acer made an equity investment in grandPad, a provider of technology solutions specifically designed for senior citizens.<sup id="cite_ref-24" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In June 2016, Acer's board of directors approved the establishment of a joint venture with <a href="/wiki/Starbreeze_Studios" title="Starbreeze Studios">Starbreeze</a> AB to design, manufacture, promote, market and sell StarVR Virtual Reality Head-Mounted Displays.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2016, Acer acquired wireless pet camera maker Pawbo.<sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2017, Acer became the largest corporate shareholder of <a href="/wiki/AOpen" title="AOpen">AOPEN</a> Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In October 2017, Acer purchased a 66.7% majority stake in StarVR from Starbreeze.<sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>In 2019, Acer and <a href="/wiki/Ubisoft" title="Ubisoft">Ubisoft</a> teamed up for the Rainbow Six Pro League and other major <a href="/wiki/Esports" title="Esports">esports</a> events with the <a href="/wiki/Acer_Predator" title="Acer Predator">Predator</a> brand as the PC and monitor sponsor.<sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Operations"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>As of September 2022, Acer has over 7,713 employees worldwide, operating in 40 countries/territories.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Australia">Australia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Australia"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Acer Computer Australia (ACA) was established in 1990, and is currently Australia's third-largest personal computer vendor, behind Hewlett-Packard Australia and <a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a> <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/New_Zealand" title="New Zealand">New Zealand</a>. ACA has Australia's highest overall market share in notebook PC and tablet PC sales. The company is also Australia's leading PC vendor in the government and education markets. As of 2006,<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> it has over 480 employees.
</p><p>The company repairs, assembles and manufacturers laptops and desktops in Sydney.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Europe">Europe</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Europe"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Acer's EMEA headquarters are located in <a href="/wiki/Lugano" title="Lugano">Lugano</a>, <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Switzerland</a>. From the late 1990s to mid-2000s, Acer had computer factories in Europe. The business area was the whole <a href="/wiki/Europe,_the_Middle_East_and_Africa" title="Europe, the Middle East and Africa">EMEA</a>. In the <a href="/wiki/Netherlands" title="Netherlands">Netherlands</a> under the name of Acer IMS bv, there were two factories: Acer laptop factory in <a href="/wiki/Den_Bosch" class="mw-redirect" title="Den Bosch">Den Bosch</a> and Acer and <a href="/wiki/IBM" title="IBM">IBM</a> desktop factory in <a href="/wiki/Tilburg" title="Tilburg">Tilburg</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> Acer also had facilities in <a href="/wiki/Germany" title="Germany">Germany</a> under the name of IMS in <a href="/wiki/Ahrensburg" title="Ahrensburg">Ahrensburg</a> and <a href="/wiki/Hamburg" title="Hamburg">Hamburg</a>. Acer computers are also assembled in <a href="/wiki/Mingachevir" title="Mingachevir">Mingachevir</a>, <a href="/wiki/Azerbaijan" title="Azerbaijan">Azerbaijan</a>.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="India">India</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: India"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>Acer's subsidiary in India is <b>Acer India (Pvt) Limited</b>, and was incorporated as a wholly-owned subsidiary of Acer Computer International, Ltd. in 1999. It is a notable vendor in key segments such as education, desktop computers, and low profile notebooks for education. The head office is in <a href="/wiki/Bengaluru" class="mw-redirect" title="Bengaluru">Bengaluru</a>, Karnataka. India rose to become Acer's second-largest worldwide market in 2023. In an effort to diversify from the computer hardware market, a new firm called AcerPure was established to sell consumer products in India.<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Indonesia">Indonesia</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Indonesia"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>PT Acer Indonesia is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Acer and distributes its products through their main distributor PT Dragon Computer &amp; Communication. Acer is currently the second-largest computer vendor in Indonesia. In the first quarter of 2016, Acer recorded &gt;81% market share in Windows tablet in Indonesia.
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="North_America">North America</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: North America"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<figure class="mw-default-size" typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:Acer-America-Offices.jpg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Acer-America-Offices.jpg/220px-Acer-America-Offices.jpg" decoding="async" width="220" height="168" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Acer-America-Offices.jpg/330px-Acer-America-Offices.jpg 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/e/ea/Acer-America-Offices.jpg/440px-Acer-America-Offices.jpg 2x" data-file-width="600" data-file-height="459" /></a><figcaption>Acer America Headquarters in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose</a></figcaption></figure>
<p>Acer America Corporation, headquartered in <a href="/wiki/San_Jose,_California" title="San Jose, California">San Jose, California</a>, is a member of the Acer Group. Acer's R&amp;D, engineering, manufacturing, and marketing operations in the United States and Canada are handled by Acer America. The U.S. headquarters was opened with a staff of three in 1985, as Multitech Electronics USA, in <a href="/wiki/Mountain_View,_California" title="Mountain View, California">Mountain View, California</a>. In 1986, the U.S. headquarters were moved to San Jose, California.
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Corporate_social_responsibility">Corporate social responsibility</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Corporate social responsibility"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Corporate_social_responsibility" title="Corporate social responsibility">Corporate social responsibility</a></div>
<p>In 2005, Acer published its first environmental report, for which the company used the <a href="/wiki/Global_Reporting_Initiative" title="Global Reporting Initiative">Global Reporting Initiative</a> (GRI) guidelines. All of Acer's tier-one suppliers have acquired <a href="/wiki/ISO_14001" class="mw-redirect" title="ISO 14001">ISO 14001</a> certification.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In November 2012, Acer was ranked 4th place out of 15 in <a href="/wiki/Greenpeace" title="Greenpeace">Greenpeace</a>'s re-launched Guide to Greener Electronics, with a score of 5.1 points out of 10. The Guide ranks electronics makers according to their policies and practices to reduce their impact on the climate, produce greener products, and make their operations more sustainable.<sup id="cite_ref-Guide_to_Greener_Electronics_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guide_to_Greener_Electronics-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Greenpeace criticized the company for not setting out targets to reduce <a href="/wiki/Greenhouse_gas" title="Greenhouse gas">greenhouse gas</a> (GHG) emissions as intended in 2010 and for not providing external verification for the GHG emissions it reports for its operations and business travel. It also scored badly on the products criteria receiving no points on product lifecycle while Greenpeace noted that a higher percentage of its products need to meet or exceed <a href="/wiki/Energy_Star" title="Energy Star">Energy Star</a> standards in order for it to score more points.<sup id="cite_ref-Guide_to_Greener_Electronics_37-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guide_to_Greener_Electronics-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>It received some praise for launching new products which are free from <a href="/wiki/Polyvinyl_chloride" title="Polyvinyl chloride">Polyvinyl chloride</a> plastic (PVC) and <a href="/wiki/Brominated_flame_retardant" title="Brominated flame retardant">brominated flame retardants</a> (BFRs) and the company informed Greenpeace that the majority of its products will be PVC/BFR free in the near future. Acer also scored well on chemical management for lobbying for restrictions on organo-halogens and was commended for reporting on GHG emissions from its first-tier suppliers and investigating its second tier.<sup id="cite_ref-Guide_to_Greener_Electronics_37-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guide_to_Greener_Electronics-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In its 2012 report on progress relating to <a href="/wiki/Conflict_minerals" class="mw-redirect" title="Conflict minerals">conflict minerals</a>, the <a href="/wiki/Enough_Project" title="Enough Project">Enough Project</a> rated Acer the seventh highest of 24 consumer electronics companies.<sup id="cite_ref-EnoughProject2012_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-EnoughProject2012-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Acer has been listed on the <a href="/wiki/Dow_Jones_Sustainability_Indices" title="Dow Jones Sustainability Indices">DJSI's</a> Emerging Markets Index since 2014 and on <a href="/wiki/MSCI_World" title="MSCI World">MSCI's</a> Global Sustainability Indexes in 2015–2016.<sup id="cite_ref-Acer_Listed_on_Global_Sustainability_Indices:_2016_DJSI_and_MSCI_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Acer_Listed_on_Global_Sustainability_Indices:_2016_DJSI_and_MSCI-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Sponsorships">Sponsorships</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=13" title="Edit section: Sponsorships"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<ul><li>Acer sponsored the <a href="/wiki/British_American_Racing" title="British American Racing">BAR</a>-<a href="/wiki/Honda_Racing_F1" class="mw-redirect" title="Honda Racing F1">Honda</a> <a href="/wiki/Formula_One" title="Formula One">Formula One</a> racing team in the year <a href="/wiki/2000_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2000 Formula One World Championship">2000</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup> In <a href="/wiki/2001_Formula_One_World_Championship" title="2001 Formula One World Championship">2001</a>, Acer provided sponsorship to the <a href="/wiki/Prost_Grand_Prix" title="Prost Grand Prix">Prost Grand Prix</a> Formula One team, and the team's <a href="/wiki/Scuderia_Ferrari" title="Scuderia Ferrari">Ferrari</a> engines were badged as Acers.</li>
<li>Acer sponsored the Ferrari Formula 1 team from 2003 up to 2012 (Official Supplier since 2006) and its Top Sponsor of FC Internazionale – Milano (Inter Milan) Football Club. From 2007 to 2009 Acer has been Official Supplier of <a href="/wiki/FC_Barcelona" title="FC Barcelona">FC Barcelona</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup> On 19 March 2007, Acer announced it would sponsor the Factory Fiat Yamaha Team for the 2007 <a href="/wiki/MotoGP" class="mw-redirect" title="MotoGP">MotoGP</a> World Championship season. Since 2009, Packard Bell (part of Acer Group) has been the sponsor of the Yamaha Factory Racing Team.<sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>Acer was the worldwide TOP Partners for both the Vancouver 2010 Olympic Winter Games and Singapore Youth Olympic Games. Acer was TOP Partner of the London <a href="/wiki/2012_Summer_Olympics" title="2012 Summer Olympics">2012 Summer Olympics</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup></li>
<li>On 18 June 2019, Acer Predator sponsored <a href="/wiki/Ubisoft" title="Ubisoft">Ubisoft</a> <a href="/wiki/Tom_Clancy%27s_Rainbow_Six_Siege" title="Tom Clancy&#39;s Rainbow Six Siege">Tom Clancy's Rainbow Six Siege</a> Pro League Season X, becoming the official PC Monitor of Rainbow Six Pro League and Majors.<sup id="cite_ref-44" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup></li></ul>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Acer_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=14" title="Edit section: Controversies"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>During the <a href="/wiki/2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" class="mw-redirect" title="2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine">2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine</a>, Acer refused to join the international community and withdraw from the Russian market. Research from <a href="/wiki/Yale_University" title="Yale University">Yale University</a> from March 28, 2022, identifying how companies were reacting to Russia's aggression identified Acer in the worst category "Digging in" meaning Defying Demands for Exit: companies defying demands for exit/reduction of activities.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> Acer subsequently reversed its decision and suspended business in Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup> Despite stating that it would suspend business in Russia, a <a href="/wiki/Reuters" title="Reuters">Reuters</a> investigation of customs data found that Acer supplied at least $70.4 million worth of computer hardware to Russia between April 8, 2022 and March 31, 2023, and it was supplied to Russia both through Acer's wholly-owned <a href="/wiki/Switzerland" title="Switzerland">Swiss</a> registered subsidiary. Since the shipments occurred outside Taiwan, they did not violate Taiwanese sanctions against Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-47" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
