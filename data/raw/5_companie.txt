<p><b>HP Inc.</b> is an <a href="/wiki/America" class="mw-redirect" title="America">American</a> <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> <a href="/wiki/Information_technology" title="Information technology">information</a> <a href="/wiki/Technology_company" title="Technology company">technology company</a> headquartered in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>, that develops <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computers</a> (PCs), <a href="/wiki/Printer_(computing)" title="Printer (computing)">printers</a> and related supplies, as well as <a href="/wiki/3D_printing" title="3D printing">3D printing</a> services. It was formed on November 1, 2015, as the legal successor of the original <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard</a> after the company's enterprise product and business services divisions were <a href="/wiki/Corporate_spin-off" title="Corporate spin-off">spun off</a> as a new publicly traded company, <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Packard_Enterprise" title="Hewlett Packard Enterprise">Hewlett Packard Enterprise</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-Darrow_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Darrow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>HP is listed on the <a href="/wiki/New_York_Stock_Exchange" title="New York Stock Exchange">New York Stock Exchange</a> and is a constituent of the <a href="/wiki/S%26P_500_Index" class="mw-redirect" title="S&amp;P 500 Index">S&amp;P 500 Index</a>. As of 2024,<sup class="plainlinks noexcerpt noprint asof-tag update" style="display:none;"><a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit">&#91;update&#93;</a></sup> it is the <a href="/wiki/Market_share_of_personal_computer_vendors" title="Market share of personal computer vendors">world's second-largest personal computer vendor</a> by unit sales after <a href="/wiki/Lenovo" title="Lenovo">Lenovo</a> and ahead of <a href="/wiki/Dell" title="Dell">Dell</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> In the 2023 <a href="/wiki/Fortune_500" title="Fortune 500"><i>Fortune</i> 500</a> list, HP is ranked 63rd-largest <a href="/wiki/United_States" title="United States">United States</a> corporation by total revenue.<sup id="cite_ref-Fortune_2023_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Fortune_2023-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup>
</p>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">Main article: <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard#History" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard §&#160;History</a></div>
<p>Hewlett-Packard was founded in 1939 by <a href="/wiki/Bill_Hewlett" title="Bill Hewlett">Bill Hewlett</a> and <a href="/wiki/David_Packard" title="David Packard">David Packard</a>, who both graduated with degrees in <a href="/wiki/Electrical_engineering" title="Electrical engineering">electrical engineering</a> from <a href="/wiki/Stanford_University" title="Stanford University">Stanford University</a> in 1935. The company started off in the <a href="/wiki/HP_Garage" title="HP Garage">HP Garage</a> in <a href="/wiki/Palo_Alto,_California" title="Palo Alto, California">Palo Alto, California</a>. On November 1, 2015, Hewlett-Packard was split into two companies. Its personal computer and printer businesses became HP Inc., while its enterprise business became <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Packard_Enterprise" title="Hewlett Packard Enterprise">Hewlett Packard Enterprise</a>. The split was structured so that Hewlett-Packard changed its name to HP Inc. and spun off Hewlett Packard Enterprise as a new publicly traded company. HP Inc. retains Hewlett-Packard's pre-2015 stock price history and its former stock ticker symbol, HPQ, while Hewlett Packard Enterprise trades under its own symbol, HPE.<sup id="cite_ref-Darrow_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Darrow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-5" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="As_HP_Inc.">As HP Inc.</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: As HP Inc."><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In May 2016, HP introduced a new <a href="/wiki/PC_gaming" class="mw-redirect" title="PC gaming">PC gaming</a> sub-brand known as <a href="/wiki/HP_Omen" title="HP Omen">Omen</a> (reusing trademarks associated with <a href="/wiki/VoodooPC" title="VoodooPC">VoodooPC</a>), including gaming laptops and desktops (with the latter offering options such as <a href="/wiki/CPU_water_cooling" class="mw-redirect" title="CPU water cooling">CPU water cooling</a> and <a href="/wiki/Nvidia" title="Nvidia">Nvidia</a>'s <a href="/wiki/GeForce_10_series" title="GeForce 10 series">GTX 1080</a> graphics, and promoted as <a href="/wiki/Virtual_reality" title="Virtual reality">VR</a>-ready), and other accessories (such as monitors) designed to cater to the market.<sup id="cite_ref-6" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In May and August 2016, certain assets were sold to <a href="/wiki/OpenText" title="OpenText">OpenText</a>, including <a href="/wiki/TeamSite" title="TeamSite">TeamSite</a> and <a href="/wiki/Exstream_Software" title="Exstream Software">Exstream</a>.
</p><p>In November 2017, HP acquired <a href="/wiki/Samsung_Electronics" title="Samsung Electronics">Samsung Electronics</a>' printer division for $1.05 billion.<sup id="cite_ref-7" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2021, HP announced its acquisition of <a href="/wiki/Kingston_Technology" title="Kingston Technology">Kingston</a>'s gaming division <a href="/wiki/HyperX" class="mw-redirect" title="HyperX">HyperX</a> for $425 million.<sup id="cite_ref-8" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-9" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> The deal only includes computer peripherals branded as HyperX, not memory or storage. The sale was completed in June 2021.<sup id="cite_ref-10" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In February 2022, HP announced it had acquired the <a href="/wiki/Edinburgh" title="Edinburgh">Edinburgh</a>-based packaging development company, Choose Packaging, in an effort to strengthen its capabilities in the sustainable packaging vertical.<sup id="cite_ref-11" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-12" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In March 2022, HP announced the acquisition of the <a href="/wiki/California" title="California">California</a>-headquartered communications software and hardware provider <a href="/wiki/Poly_Inc." title="Poly Inc.">Poly Inc.</a> in an all-cash transaction. HP said the cash amount agreed was $40 per share, which implied a total enterprise value of $3.3bn, inclusive Poly's net debts.<sup id="cite_ref-13" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Attempted_merger_with_Xerox">Attempted merger with Xerox</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Attempted merger with Xerox"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>On November 5, 2019, <i><a href="/wiki/The_Wall_Street_Journal" title="The Wall Street Journal">The Wall Street Journal</a></i> reported that print and digital document company <a href="/wiki/Xerox" title="Xerox">Xerox</a> was contemplating acquiring HP.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup> The company unanimously rejected two unsolicited offers, including a cash-and-stock offer at $22 per-share.<sup id="cite_ref-15" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> HP stated that there was "uncertainty regarding Xerox’s ability to raise the cash portion of the proposed consideration" (especially given that Xerox is a smaller company in terms of market cap than HP), and noted the company's aggressiveness.<sup id="cite_ref-:0_16-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:0-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup> On November 26, 2019, Xerox issued a public letter defending allegations by HP that its offer was "uncertain" and "highly conditional", and declared its intent to "engage directly with HP shareholders to solicit their support in urging the HP Board to do the right thing and pursue this compelling opportunity."<sup id="cite_ref-17" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Xerox stated in January 2020 that it would propose the replacement of HP's board of directors during its next shareholder meeting in April 2020. In a statement to <i>TechCrunch</i>, HP disclosed a belief that Xerox's bid was being "driven by" <a href="/wiki/Activist_shareholder" class="mw-redirect" title="Activist shareholder">activist shareholder</a> <a href="/wiki/Carl_Icahn" title="Carl Icahn">Carl Icahn</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-18" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup> Xerox raised its bid to $24 per-share in February 2020.<sup id="cite_ref-19" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On February 21, 2020, HP instituted a <a href="/wiki/Shareholder_rights_plan" title="Shareholder rights plan">shareholder rights plan</a> to fend off Xerox's pursuit of a <a href="/wiki/Takeover#Hostile" title="Takeover">hostile takeover</a>. Four days later, HP announced that, if shareholders rejected the Xerox purchase, it planned on offering $16 billion in capital return between fiscal 2020 and 2022, including $8 billion in additional share buybacks and raising its "target long-term return of capital to 100% of free cash flow generation". HP criticized Xerox's bid as a "flawed value exchange" based on "overstated synergies".<sup id="cite_ref-20" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-21" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup> On March 5, 2020, HP rejected an offer at $24 per-share.<sup id="cite_ref-22" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On March 31, 2020, Xerox rescinded its bid to buy HP Inc, citing that "the <a href="/wiki/COVID-19_pandemic" title="COVID-19 pandemic">current global health crisis</a> and <a href="/wiki/Economic_impact_of_the_COVID-19_pandemic" title="Economic impact of the COVID-19 pandemic">resulting macroeconomic and market turmoil</a>" had "created an environment that is not conducive to Xerox continuing to pursue an acquisition of HP Inc."<sup id="cite_ref-23" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Products_and_operations">Products and operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=4" title="Edit section: Products and operations"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/List_of_assets_owned_by_HP" title="List of assets owned by HP">List of assets owned by HP</a></div>
<figure typeof="mw:File/Thumb"><a href="/wiki/File:HP_Logo_2017.svg" class="mw-file-description"><img src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/HP_Logo_2017.svg/100px-HP_Logo_2017.svg.png" decoding="async" width="100" height="166" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/HP_Logo_2017.svg/150px-HP_Logo_2017.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/commons/thumb/b/b7/HP_Logo_2017.svg/201px-HP_Logo_2017.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="701" data-file-height="1159" /></a><figcaption>Alternative HP logo used on some computers since 2016</figcaption></figure>
<p>HP develops <a href="/wiki/Personal_computer" title="Personal computer">personal computers</a> (PCs), <a href="/wiki/Printer_(computing)" title="Printer (computing)">printers</a> and related supplies, as well as <a href="/wiki/3D_printing" title="3D printing">3D printing</a> services.<sup id="cite_ref-Darrow_2-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Darrow-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2023_Form_10-K_1-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2023_Form_10-K-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page: 5–6">&#58;&#8202;5–6&#8202;</span></sup> HP's product lines include the <a href="/wiki/HP_Pavilion" title="HP Pavilion">HP Pavilion</a>, <a href="/wiki/HP_Envy" title="HP Envy">Envy</a>, <a href="/wiki/HP_Omen" title="HP Omen">Omen</a>, <a href="/wiki/HP_Essential" title="HP Essential">Essential</a> and <a href="/wiki/Chromebook" title="Chromebook">Chromebook</a> PCs, as well as the <a href="/wiki/DeskJet" class="mw-redirect" title="DeskJet">DeskJet</a>, OfficeJet, and <a href="/wiki/LaserJet" class="mw-redirect" title="LaserJet">LaserJet</a> series of printers.
</p><p>In March 2015, HP announced that <a href="/wiki/Bang_%26_Olufsen" title="Bang &amp; Olufsen">Bang &amp; Olufsen</a> would become the company's new premium audio partner for its computers and other devices. This replaced the partnership with <a href="/wiki/Beats_Electronics" title="Beats Electronics">Beats Electronics</a> that ended upon being acquired by <a href="/wiki/Apple_Inc." title="Apple Inc.">Apple Inc.</a> in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-cnet-bando_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-cnet-bando-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup> 
</p><p>In fiscal year 2023, total revenue of <span style="white-space: nowrap"><a href="/wiki/United_States_dollar" title="United States dollar">US$</a>54 billion</span> included US$24.7 billion from the sale of <a href="/wiki/Laptop" title="Laptop">notebook computers</a>, US$10.9 billion from the sale of <a href="/wiki/Desktop_computer" title="Desktop computer">desktop computers</a>, US$11.4 billion from the sale of printer supplies, US$4.2 billion from the sale of commercial printers, and US$2.4 billion from the sale of consumer printers.<sup id="cite_ref-2023_Form_10-K_1-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2023_Form_10-K-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page: 40–41">&#58;&#8202;40–41&#8202;</span></sup> Over 65 percent of revenue in 2022 came from customers outside of the United States.<sup id="cite_ref-2023_Form_10-K_1-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2023_Form_10-K-1">&#91;1&#93;</a></sup><sup class="reference nowrap"><span title="Page: 13">&#58;&#8202;13&#8202;</span></sup> 
</p><p>HP's first quarter fiscal 2024 net revenue was $13.2 billion, representing a 4.4% decrease (4.9% in constant currency) year-over-year.<sup id="cite_ref-25" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-26" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup> HP's strong operating activities in the first quarter of fiscal 2024 generated $121 million in net cash. The company also reported a positive free cash flow of $25 million for the quarter.<sup id="cite_ref-27" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-28" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-29" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup> Free cash flow includes $121 million in net cash from operating activities, with adjustments of $62 million for net lease investments and $158 million for property, plant, and equipment investments.<sup id="cite_ref-30" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-30">&#91;30&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Controversies">Controversies</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=5" title="Edit section: Controversies"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">This article is about the company created in 2015. For information about controversies concerning the predecessor company, see <a href="/wiki/Hewlett-Packard#Controversies" title="Hewlett-Packard">Hewlett-Packard §&#160;Controversies</a>.</div>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Blocking_third-party_ink_cartridges">Blocking third-party ink cartridges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=6" title="Edit section: Blocking third-party ink cartridges"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2016, HP introduced firmware in its printers that disabled the printers if users used ink or toner cartridges which did not contain “new or reused HP chips or electronic circuitry.” As a result, HP faced scathing criticism (such as that from the <a href="/wiki/Electronic_Frontier_Foundation" title="Electronic Frontier Foundation">Electronic Frontier Foundation</a><sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>) and paid millions in class-action lawsuits, such as to certain customers in the US ($1.5 million<sup id="cite_ref-32" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup>), Canada ($700,000 CAD<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>), Australia, and Europe ($1.350 million<sup id="cite_ref-34" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup>), but without admitting wrongdoing. HP's stated that the firmware was intended to provide "the best consumer experience" and "protect" customers from "counterfeit and third-party ink cartridges that do not contain an original HP security chip and that infringe on our <a href="/wiki/Intellectual_property" title="Intellectual property">IP</a>."<sup id="cite_ref-35" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2023, <i><a href="/wiki/PC_World" title="PC World">PC World</a></i> reported that HP printers still prevent users from using third-party ink.<sup id="cite_ref-36" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Disabling_all-in-one_printers">Disabling all-in-one printers</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=7" title="Edit section: Disabling all-in-one printers"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2023, HP was sued over allegations that it intentionally designed its all-in-one printers not to perform scanning or faxing when they were low on ink.<sup id="cite_ref-37" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Disabling_ink_cartridges">Disabling ink cartridges</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=8" title="Edit section: Disabling ink cartridges"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In February 2019, How-To Geek published an article reporting that HP remotely disabled ink cartridges when the user cancels their Instant Ink subscription.<sup id="cite_ref-38" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Loss_of_company_archives_under_Keysight">Loss of company archives under Keysight</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=9" title="Edit section: Loss of company archives under Keysight"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>A large part of Hewlett-Packard's historical company archives (consisting of over 100 boxes of correspondence and documents from the two founders) were acquired by <a href="/wiki/Keysight" title="Keysight">Keysight Technologies</a> at the time of its foundation in 2014.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> These archives were completely lost when the 2017 <a href="/wiki/Tubbs_Fire" title="Tubbs Fire">Tubbs wildfire</a> incinerated two buildings on the headquarters campus of Keysight in Santa Rosa, California.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_39-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup> A former HP employee who had previously been in charge of the archives commented that "a huge piece of American business history is gone", and Keysight disputed criticism that the archives had been inadequately protected.<sup id="cite_ref-:10_39-2" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:10-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Xinjiang_region">Xinjiang region</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=10" title="Edit section: Xinjiang region"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1033289096"><div role="note" class="hatnote navigation-not-searchable">See also: <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang_internment_camps" title="Xinjiang internment camps">Xinjiang internment camps</a> and <a href="/wiki/Persecution_of_Uyghurs_in_China" title="Persecution of Uyghurs in China">Persecution of Uyghurs in China</a></div>
<p>In 2020, the <a href="/wiki/Australian_Strategic_Policy_Institute" title="Australian Strategic Policy Institute">Australian Strategic Policy Institute</a> accused at least 82 major brands, including HP Inc, of being potentially connected to forced <a href="/wiki/Uyghurs" title="Uyghurs">Uyghur</a> labor in the Chinese <a href="/wiki/Xinjiang" title="Xinjiang">Xinjiang</a> province.<sup id="cite_ref-40" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2022, in response to the U.S. <a href="/wiki/Uyghur_Forced_Labor_Prevention_Act" title="Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act">Uyghur Forced Labor Prevention Act</a>, HP responded, "HP is committed to respecting human rights across our global supply chain and we prohibit the use of involuntary labor of any kind. HP also commits to respecting fundamental rights and freedoms defined in the <a href="/wiki/Universal_Declaration_of_Human_Rights" title="Universal Declaration of Human Rights">United Nations (UN) Universal Declaration of Human Rights</a> (UDHR) in an approach consistent with the <a href="/wiki/United_Nations_Guiding_Principles_on_Business_and_Human_Rights" title="United Nations Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights">UN Guiding Principles on Business and Human Rights</a> (UNGPs) as outlined in our Human Rights Policy."<sup id="cite_ref-41" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-42" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h3><span class="mw-headline" id="Israeli_conflicts">Israeli conflicts</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=11" title="Edit section: Israeli conflicts"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h3>
<p>In 2023, HP as well as <a href="/wiki/Hewlett_Packard_Enterprise" title="Hewlett Packard Enterprise">Hewlett Packard Enterprise</a> (HPE) has come under criticism for its products provided to the <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Police" class="mw-redirect" title="Israeli Police">Israeli Police</a>, <a href="/wiki/Israeli_Prison_Service" class="mw-redirect" title="Israeli Prison Service">Israeli Prison Service</a>, and <a href="/wiki/Population_and_Immigration_Authority" title="Population and Immigration Authority">Israeli Population and Immigration Authority</a>. The BDS movement has called for consumers and organizations to boycott all HPE IT services and products as well as printers, computers, and printer cartridges from HP Inc.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Carbon_footprint">Carbon footprint</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit&amp;section=12" title="Edit section: Carbon footprint"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<link rel="mw-deduplicated-inline-style" href="mw-data:TemplateStyles:r1097763485"><table class="box-Cleanup_press_release plainlinks metadata ambox ambox-content" role="presentation"><tbody><tr><td class="mbox-image"><div class="mbox-image-div"><span typeof="mw:File"><span><img alt="" src="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/40px-Ambox_important.svg.png" decoding="async" width="40" height="40" class="mw-file-element" srcset="//upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/60px-Ambox_important.svg.png 1.5x, //upload.wikimedia.org/wikipedia/en/thumb/b/b4/Ambox_important.svg/80px-Ambox_important.svg.png 2x" data-file-width="40" data-file-height="40" /></span></span></div></td><td class="mbox-text"><div class="mbox-text-span">This article <b>reads like <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not#PROMO" title="Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not">a press release</a> or <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:What_Wikipedia_is_not#NEWS" title="Wikipedia:What Wikipedia is not">a news article</a></b> and may be <b>largely based on <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Notability_(events)#Routine_coverage" title="Wikipedia:Notability (events)">routine coverage</a></b>.<span class="hide-when-compact"> Please help <a class="external text" href="https://en.wikipedia.org/w/index.php?title=HP_Inc.&amp;action=edit">improve this article</a> and add <a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Independent_sources" title="Wikipedia:Independent sources">independent sources</a>.</span></div></td></tr></tbody></table>
<p>HP reported Total <a href="/wiki/Carbon_footprint" title="Carbon footprint">CO<sub>2</sub>e emissions</a> (Direct + Indirect) for the twelve months ending December 31, 2020, at 254 Kt (−34 /-11.8% y-o-y)<sup id="cite_ref-HP_Inc._Total_CO2e_emissions_for_2020Q4_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-HP_Inc._Total_CO2e_emissions_for_2020Q4-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> and plans to reduce emissions 60% by 2025 from a 2015 base year.<sup id="cite_ref-45" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> This <a href="/wiki/Science_Based_Targets_initiative" title="Science Based Targets initiative">science-based target</a> is aligned with <a href="/wiki/Paris_Agreement" title="Paris Agreement">Paris Agreement</a> to limit global warming to 1.5&#160;°C above pre-industrial levels.<sup id="cite_ref-46" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>According to a press release issued on April 20, 2021,<sup id="cite_ref-:1_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> HP seeks to achieve net zero greenhouse gas emissions across the HP value chain by 2040, beginning with their Supplies business achieving <a href="/wiki/Carbon_neutrality" class="mw-redirect" title="Carbon neutrality">carbon neutrality</a> by 2030. "Climate change is a defining challenge of our generation that demands immediate action and investment," said Enrique Lores, HP Inc. President and CEO. "Now is a time for bold moves and ambitious goals that will protect our planet and create new sources of innovation and growth across the global economy. By driving toward net-zero carbon emissions across our entire value chain by 2040, product circularity and a forest positive framework, we will accelerate our progress and advance HP’s aspiration to become the world’s most sustainable and just technology company."<sup id="cite_ref-:1_47-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-:1-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
