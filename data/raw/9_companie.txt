<p><b>Micro-Star International Co., Ltd.</b> (commonly known as <b>MSI</b>; <a href="/wiki/Chinese_language" title="Chinese language">Chinese</a>&#58; <span lang="zh">微星科技股份有限公司</span>) is a Taiwanese <a href="/wiki/Multinational_corporation" title="Multinational corporation">multinational</a> information technology corporation headquartered in <a href="/wiki/New_Taipei_City" title="New Taipei City">New Taipei City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>. It designs, develops and provides <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computer</a> hardware as well as related products and services, including laptops, desktops, motherboards, graphics cards, all-in-one PCs, <a href="/wiki/Server_(computing)" title="Server (computing)">servers</a>, industrial computers, PC <a href="/wiki/Peripheral" title="Peripheral">peripherals</a>, and car <a href="/wiki/Infotainment" title="Infotainment">infotainment</a> products, among other products.
</p><p>The company has a primary listing on the <a href="/wiki/Taiwan_Stock_Exchange" title="Taiwan Stock Exchange">Taiwan Stock Exchange</a><sup id="cite_ref-stock_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-stock-2">&#91;2&#93;</a></sup> and was established on August 4, 1986<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3">&#91;3&#93;</a></sup> by five founders – Hsu Xiang (a.k.a. Joseph Hsu),<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> Huang Jinqing (a.k.a. Jeans Huang),<sup id="cite_ref-JeansHuang_4-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JeansHuang-4">&#91;4&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-JeansHuang2_5-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-JeansHuang2-5">&#91;5&#93;</a></sup> Lin Wentong (a.k.a. Frank Lin),<sup id="cite_ref-FrankLin_6-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-FrankLin-6">&#91;6&#93;</a></sup> Yu Xian'nengyu (a.k.a. Kenny Yu),<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. Henry Lu).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (August 2021)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> First starting its business in <a href="/wiki/New_Taipei_City" title="New Taipei City">New Taipei City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, MSI later expanded into <a href="/wiki/Mainland_China" title="Mainland China">China</a>, setting up its <a href="/wiki/Bao%27an_District" class="mw-redirect" title="Bao&#39;an District">Bao'an</a> plant in <a href="/wiki/Shenzhen" title="Shenzhen">Shenzhen</a> in 2000 and establishing <a href="/wiki/Research_and_development" title="Research and development">research and development</a> facilities in <a href="/wiki/Kunshan" title="Kunshan">Kunshan</a> in 2001. It also provides global warranty service in North America, Central/South America, Asia, Australia and Europe.

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="Operations">Operations</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micro-Star_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Operations"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MSI's offices in <a href="/wiki/Zhonghe_District" title="Zhonghe District">Zhonghe District</a>, <a href="/wiki/New_Taipei_City" title="New Taipei City">New Taipei City</a>, <a href="/wiki/Taiwan" title="Taiwan">Taiwan</a>, serve as the company's headquarters, and house a number of different divisions and services.<sup id="cite_ref-headquarters_7-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-headquarters-7">&#91;7&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Manufacturing initially took place at plants in Taiwan, but has been moved elsewhere. Many MSI graphics cards are manufactured at its plant in mainland China.
</p><p>The company has branch offices in the <a href="/wiki/Americas" title="Americas">Americas</a>, <a href="/wiki/Europe" title="Europe">Europe</a>, <a href="/wiki/Asia" title="Asia">Asia</a>, <a href="/wiki/Australia" title="Australia">Australia</a> and <a href="/wiki/South_Africa" title="South Africa">South Africa</a>. As of 2015, the company has a global presence in over 120 countries.

<p>The company first built its reputation on developing and manufacturing <a href="/wiki/Computer" title="Computer">computer</a> <a href="/wiki/Motherboard" title="Motherboard">motherboards</a> and graphics cards. It established its subsidiary <a href="/wiki/FUNTORO" title="FUNTORO">FUNTORO</a> in 2008 for the vehicle <a href="/wiki/Infotainment" title="Infotainment">infotainment</a> market. It provides many computer and tech oriented products including <a href="/wiki/Laptop" title="Laptop">laptops</a>, <a href="/wiki/Desktop_computer" title="Desktop computer">desktops</a>, <a href="/wiki/Computer_monitor" title="Computer monitor">monitors</a>, motherboards, graphics cards, power supply products, computer cases and liquid cooler for gamers and content creators, all-in-one PCs, mobile workstations, servers, IPCs, multimedia peripherals, vehicle infotainment, and an autonomous mobility robot.<sup id="cite_ref-AMR_8-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AMR-8">&#91;8&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>When established in 1986, MSI focused on the design and manufacturing of motherboards and add-on cards. Later that year, it introduced the first over-clockable <a href="/wiki/Intel_80286" title="Intel 80286">286</a> motherboard.
</p><p>In 1989, MSI introduced its first <a href="/wiki/Intel_80486" class="mw-redirect" title="Intel 80486">486</a> motherboard; in 1990, its first <a href="/wiki/Socket_7" title="Socket 7">Socket 7</a> based motherboard; in 1993, its first 586 motherboard; and in 1995, its Dual Pentium Pro-based motherboard. In 1997, it introduced its Intel <a href="/wiki/Pentium_II" title="Pentium II">Pentium II</a>-based motherboard with Intel MMX Technology, along with its first graphics card product and first barebone product. In 2002, it released its first PC2PC Bluetooth &amp; WLAN motherboard.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (April 2020)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2000, MSI introduced its first <a href="/wiki/Set-top_box" title="Set-top box">set-top box</a> product (MS-5205);<sup id="cite_ref-MS-5205_9-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MS-5205-9">&#91;9&#93;</a></sup> in 2003, its first Pen Tablet PC product (PenNote3100);<sup id="cite_ref-PenNote3100_10-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-PenNote3100-10">&#91;10&#93;</a></sup> and in 2004, its first Notebook product (M510C).<sup id="cite_ref-M510C_11-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-M510C-11">&#91;11&#93;</a></sup> In 2009, MSI introduced its first Ultra Slim Notebook (X320),<sup id="cite_ref-X320_12-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-X320-12">&#91;12&#93;</a></sup> and first all-in-one PC (AP1900).<sup id="cite_ref-AP1900_13-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AP1900-13">&#91;13&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>MSI released their first <a href="/wiki/Digital_audio_player" class="mw-redirect" title="Digital audio player">digital audio player</a> in 2003, in a line called MEGA.<sup id="cite_ref-14" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-14">&#91;14&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2008, MSI sponsored <a href="/wiki/Fnatic" title="Fnatic">Fnatic</a> and dived into the PC gaming market. Its GAMING series features laptops, desktops, <a href="/wiki/Motherboard" title="Motherboard">motherboards</a>, graphic cards, all-in-one PCs and gaming <a href="/wiki/Peripheral" title="Peripheral">peripherals</a> designed for gamers and power users.
</p><p>The company has been a sponsor for a number of <a href="/wiki/Esports" title="Esports">eSports</a> teams<sup id="cite_ref-MSI_Gaming_15-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MSI_Gaming-15">&#91;15&#93;</a></sup> and is also the host of the international gaming event MSI Masters Gaming Arena (formerly known as MSI Beat IT).<sup id="cite_ref-joinDOTA_16-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-joinDOTA-16">&#91;16&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-geforce_17-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-geforce-17">&#91;17&#93;</a></sup> The earliest Beat IT tournament can be traced back to 2010, featuring <a href="/wiki/Evil_Geniuses" title="Evil Geniuses">Evil Geniuses</a> winning the championship.<sup id="cite_ref-BEATIT2010_18-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-BEATIT2010-18">&#91;18&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2015, MSI teamed up with eye-tracking tech firm <a href="/wiki/Tobii_Technology" class="mw-redirect" title="Tobii Technology">Tobii</a> for the creation of eye-tracking gaming laptops.<sup id="cite_ref-Tobii1_19-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tobii1-19">&#91;19&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Tobii2_20-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Tobii2-20">&#91;20&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In early 2016, MSI announced a collaboration with <a href="/wiki/HTC" title="HTC">HTC</a> and has revealed Vive-ready systems to offer Virtual Reality experiences.<sup id="cite_ref-VR1_21-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VR1-21">&#91;21&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VR2_22-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VR2-22">&#91;22&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VR3_23-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VR3-23">&#91;23&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VR4_24-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VR4-24">&#91;24&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VR5_25-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VR5-25">&#91;25&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-VR6_26-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-VR6-26">&#91;26&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>MSI expanded its scope of business into Content Creation in 2018 and demonstrated creator-centric laptops at IFA 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-Creation1_27-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Creation1-27">&#91;27&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Creation2_28-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Creation2-28">&#91;28&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Creation3_29-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Creation3-29">&#91;29&#93;</a></sup>

<h2><span class="mw-headline" id="History">History</span><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=Micro-Star_International&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: History"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></h2>
<p>MSI's five founders Joseph Hsu, Jeans Huang, Frank Lin, Kenny Yu and Henry Lu all worked for <a href="/wiki/Sony" title="Sony">Sony</a> before establishing MSI. Sony's corporate downsizing in 1985 brought them together. With the engineering background working for Sony, they established Micro Star International together in August 1986.
</p><p>In 1997, MSI inaugurated Plant I in Zhonghe, followed by the opening of Plant III in Zhonghe in 2000. In the same year, MSI Computer (Shenzhen) Co., Ltd. was established, and in 2001, MSI Electronics (Kunshan) Co., Ltd. was founded. In 2002, MSI set up its European logistics center in the Netherlands. The company went public in 1998 with an IPO on the Taipei Stock Exchange (TAIEX).<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (February 2023)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup>
</p><p>In 2003, MSi released the "Mega PC", a <a href="/wiki/Shelf_stereo" class="mw-redirect" title="Shelf stereo">shelf stereo</a> computer hybrid with a front panel resembling the former and desktop computer connectors on the rear.<sup id="cite_ref-31" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-31">&#91;31&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In 2017, MSI signed a laptop distribution deal with Mustek.<sup id="cite_ref-MUSTEKMSI_32-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MUSTEKMSI-32">&#91;32&#93;</a></sup> In April of the same year, MSI names certified partners for the creation of an RGB ecosystem with MSI Mystic Light Sync which includes <a href="/wiki/Corsair_Gaming" title="Corsair Gaming">Corsair</a>, <a href="/wiki/SteelSeries" title="SteelSeries">SteelSeries</a>, <a href="/wiki/G.Skill" title="G.Skill">G.Skill</a>, <a href="/wiki/Cooler_Master" title="Cooler Master">Cooler Master</a>, <a href="/wiki/InWin" class="mw-redirect" title="InWin">InWin</a>, <a href="/wiki/Phanteks" title="Phanteks">Phanteks</a>, and others.<sup id="cite_ref-33" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-33">&#91;33&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Also April 2017, MSI launched "Join the Dragon" team sponsorship program to discover talented eSports teams.<sup id="cite_ref-jointhedragon_34-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-jointhedragon-34">&#91;34&#93;</a></sup> They have partnered with <a href="/wiki/ESL_(company)" title="ESL (company)">ESL</a> for ESL One events in 2018<sup id="cite_ref-ESL2018_35-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESL2018-35">&#91;35&#93;</a></sup> and became the official partner of ESL One Cologne 2018, one of the biggest events on the <a href="/wiki/Counter-Strike:_Global_Offensive" title="Counter-Strike: Global Offensive">Counter-Strike: Global Offensive</a> calendar.<sup id="cite_ref-ESLCologne2018_36-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-ESLCologne2018-36">&#91;36&#93;</a></sup> MSI sponsored the Method eSports organization in August 2018.<sup id="cite_ref-Method_37-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Method-37">&#91;37&#93;</a></sup> MSI partnered with ESL to bring MGA 2018 grand finals to New York. Kazakhstan's AVANGAR won the Championship.<sup id="cite_ref-MGA_2018_38-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-MGA_2018-38">&#91;38&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-AVANGAR_39-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-AVANGAR-39">&#91;39&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>MSI and Ubisoft jointly presented Ambient Link (synchronized game lighting) on Assassin's Creed Odyssey and Tom Clancy's The Division 2 in 2019.<sup id="cite_ref-Ambient_Link1_40-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ambient_Link1-40">&#91;40&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ambient_Link2_41-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ambient_Link2-41">&#91;41&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-Ambient_Link3_42-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Ambient_Link3-42">&#91;42&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>In September 2020, MSI unveiled a new line of business-oriented laptops under the "Modern", "Prestige", and "Summit" lines, and a new logo specific to these models.<sup id="cite_ref-43" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-43">&#91;43&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>Over the years, MSI has earned awards and recognitions, including being ranked among the Top 20 Taiwan Global Brands in 2008<sup id="cite_ref-2008TaiwanGlobalBrands_44-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2008TaiwanGlobalBrands-44">&#91;44&#93;</a></sup> and named one of the Top 100 Taiwan Brands in 2011.<sup id="cite_ref-Top100_45-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Top100-45">&#91;45&#93;</a></sup> In 2015, Laptop magazine ranked MSI the fourth-best laptop brand of the year,<sup id="cite_ref-laptopmag1_46-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laptopmag1-46">&#91;46&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-laptopmag2_47-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-laptopmag2-47">&#91;47&#93;</a></sup> and according to Topology Research Institute, MSI was the largest gaming laptop supplier worldwide in 2016.<sup id="cite_ref-2016research1_48-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2016research1-48">&#91;48&#93;</a></sup><sup id="cite_ref-2016research2_49-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-2016research2-49">&#91;49&#93;</a></sup> MSI had also been awarded the Taiwan Excellence award for 15 consecutive years in 2013.<sup id="cite_ref-TaiwanExcellence_50-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-TaiwanExcellence-50">&#91;50&#93;</a></sup> In August 2018, MSI was rated the Best Gaming Laptop Brand of 2018 by Laptop Mag. New designs of its GS65 Stealth Thin and GE63 Raider RGB laptops earned the company an 84 out of 100 and put it on the top spot.<sup id="cite_ref-LaptopMag2018_51-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-LaptopMag2018-51">&#91;51&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>On 7 October 2020, MSI released a public statement about their subsidiary Starlit scalping MSI-made <a href="/wiki/Nvidia" title="Nvidia">Nvidia</a> <a href="/wiki/GeForce_30_series" title="GeForce 30 series">RTX 3080 and 3090</a> <a href="/wiki/Graphics_processing_unit" title="Graphics processing unit">GPUs</a>, and selling them for higher than MSRP on <a href="/wiki/EBay" title="EBay">eBay</a>.<sup id="cite_ref-52" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-52">&#91;52&#93;</a></sup>
</p><p>During the <a href="/wiki/2022_Russian_invasion_of_Ukraine" class="mw-redirect" title="2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine">2022 Russian invasion of Ukraine</a>, MSI refused to withdraw from the Russian market. Research by <a href="/wiki/Jeffrey_Sonnenfeld" title="Jeffrey Sonnenfeld">Jeffrey Sonnenfeld</a> of Yale University put MSI in the <a href="/wiki/Yale_CELI_List_of_Companies" title="Yale CELI List of Companies">Yale CELI List of Companies</a> category of "Digging In - Defying Demands for Exit or Reduction of Activities", the only Taiwanese company in the database.<sup id="cite_ref-53" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-53">&#91;53&#93;</a></sup> MSI issued a statement on March 28, 2022, claiming that it abides by all international regulations in terms of its sales and operations in Russia.<sup id="cite_ref-54" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-54">&#91;54&#93;</a></sup>
</p>
